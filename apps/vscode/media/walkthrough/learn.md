# Learn the basics

Team Topologies models an organisation as **teams** and the **interactions** between them.

**The four team types**

- **Stream-aligned** — delivers a continuous flow of value for a business domain.
- **Enabling** — helps other teams adopt new skills or technologies.
- **Complicated-subsystem** — owns a part that needs deep specialist knowledge.
- **Platform** — provides internal services that reduce cognitive load for stream-aligned teams.

**The three interaction modes**

- **Collaboration** — two teams work closely together for a while.
- **X-as-a-Service** — one team consumes something another team provides.
- **Facilitating** — one team helps another for a defined period.

**Working in the editor**

- Drag a team type from the palette onto the canvas, then click its label to rename it.
- Drag an interaction mode from the palette and drop it over the teams it connects.
- Select an element to get its context pad, which renames or deletes it.

Your changes are saved straight back into the `.tt` file, so diagrams version nicely in Git.

New to the method? Start with [teamtopologies.com](https://teamtopologies.com/).
